Battle of Saragarhi

A tale of courage and Valour

History is full of stories of great valour displayed by some individuals. This is tribute to a group of 21 Soldiers (comprising of 3 non-commissioned officers and 18 other ranks of the 36th Sikhs of the British Indian Army) who displayed exemplary courage and valour in fighiting against all odds faced a massive 10,000 and 14,000 Pashtuns.
They held onto their signalling post long enough and thereby delayed the Pashtuns for too long so that the reinforcements could arrive in the neighbouring fort. Due to this the element of surprise that the Pashtun fighters had over the British army was neutralised.

The battle of Saragarhi is often compared to the battle of Thermopayle which took place in 480 BC in which a group of 300 greek soldiers faced the massive Persian army.
